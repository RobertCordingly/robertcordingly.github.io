About

I'm a PhD student at the University of Washington Tacoma, advised by Prof. Wes Lloyd. My research builds tools and models that make serverless computing more observable, predictable, and portable — so that developers can reason about cost, performance, and infrastructure trade-offs as easily as they reason about code.

Across roughly twenty publications I've worked on serverless performance modeling, Function-as-a-Service observability, multi-cloud "sky" computing, ARM vs. x86 platform characterization, container runtime isolation, and the carbon and cost implications of global workload distribution. I'm also a Predoctoral Instructor in the Graduate Certificate in Software Development Engineering program, where I've taught TCSS 501 and TCSS 505 with an average evaluation of 4.6/5.

I completed my M.S. (2020) and B.S. (2018, Cum Laude) in Computer Science & Systems at UW Tacoma. Outside of research I build video games — one of them, Decks of Dexterity, has accumulated over a million plays on GX Games.

Open-Source Projects

SAAF — Serverless Application Analytics Framework

A Function-as-a-Service profiling framework for Node.js, Python, and Java functions deployed to AWS, Google Cloud, IBM Cloud, and Azure. Captures fine-grained runtime metrics that enable platform performance evaluation and cost / performance modeling.

github.com/wlloyduw/SAAF · Tutorial

FaaSET — Function-as-a-Service Experiment Toolkit

A Python framework that integrates with Jupyter notebooks to streamline serverless development — deployment, testing, benchmarking, and analysis from a single workspace. Used in tutorials and demos.

Open in Google Colab

FaaS Runner

A client application that creates and automates reproducible experiments across Function-as-a-Service cloud platforms. Recently extended into Distributed FaaSRunner for multi-node, multi-threaded endpoint testing (IC2E 2025).

github.com/wlloyduw/SAAF/test

EasyRL Cloud

Cloud deployment for EasyRL — a framework for training, testing, and experimenting with reinforcement learning models.

github.com/easyRL/easyRL-v0
